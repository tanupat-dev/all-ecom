# An Accounting Entry holds signed income AND fee lines that sum to the net payout вҖ” not fees alone

An Order's Accounting Entry is the **full settlement breakdown** the Platform reports for that Order: a positive **income** line (the gross sale, plus any buyer-paid shipping income), one negative line per **fee** the Platform deducted, and any **refund** line вҖ” every line a signed integer-satang amount under one `AccountingLineCategory`. **Actual Net = the sum of all of the Order's lines across all cycles**, and that sum equals the amount the Platform actually transferred to the seller's balance. The accounting importer therefore reads the gross-sale and income columns as lines too вҖ” not only the fee columns вҖ” and **fail-loud cross-checks** the line sum against the file's own stated transferred-total column (ADR 0005): a mismatch means a column was missed or misread, and the row is held, never silently accepted.

This supersedes the narrower reading of "Fee Category" as a fee-only taxonomy. The enum is renamed `FeeCategory` вҶ’ **`AccountingLineCategory`** (it spans income and deductions; it matches the model name `AccountingEntryLine`), and gains the income-side buckets `sale_income` and `refund`. The cycle-aware idempotency of ADR 0007 is unchanged вҖ” this ADR only pins *what the lines are*, not *how re-import dedupes them*.

## Why

- **It is the cross-industry standard for settlementвҶ’ledger.** Stripe's `BalanceTransaction` models a gross `amount` (the income, a first-class positive value) separately from `fee` / `fee_details[]`, with `net = amount вҲ’ fee`; the customer payment is a positive line and each fee a negative line. A2X вҖ” the de-facto marketplace-settlement-to-GL connector for Shopify/Amazon вҖ” decomposes every settlement into *separate GL accounts* (revenue, refunds, referral/marketplace fees, fulfilment, advertising, sales tax, reimbursements) whose signed lines sum to the net payout, and it **splits a settlement that straddles a period boundary into two entries** вҖ” the same shape as our statement-cycle replace boundary (ADR 0007). Both put income as its own line in its own account, never bucketed among fees.
- **It is what the real export files contain.** Every Platform's accounting export carries the gross sale in the same row as the fees: Shopee Income `аёӘаёҙаёҷаё„а№үаёІаёЈаёІаё„аёІаёӣаёҒаё•аёҙ` (+) alongside `аё„а№ҲаёІаё„аёӯаёЎаёЎаёҙаёҠаёҠаёұа№Ҳаёҷ` / `аё„а№ҲаёІаёҡаёЈаёҙаёҒаёІаёЈ` / `аё„а№ҲаёІаёҳаёёаёЈаёҒаёЈаёЈаёЎаёҒаёІаёЈаёҠаёіаёЈаё°а№ҖаёҮаёҙаёҷ` (вҲ’) and a `аёҲаёіаёҷаё§аёҷа№ҖаёҮаёҙаёҷаё—аёұа№үаёҮаё«аёЎаё”аё—аёөа№Ҳа№Ӯаёӯаёҷа№ҒаёҘа№үаё§ (аёҝ)` total; Lazada's journal posts `аёўаёӯаё”аёЈаё§аёЎаё„а№ҲаёІаёӘаёҙаёҷаё„а№үаёІ` (+) and `аё«аёұаёҒаё„а№ҲаёІаёҳаёЈаёЈаёЎа№ҖаёҷаёөаёўаёЎвҖҰ` (вҲ’) as sibling rows under one `аёЈаё«аёұаёӘаёЈаёӯаёҡаёҡаёҙаёҘ`; TikTok's wide row carries `аёЈаёІаёўа№„аё”а№үаёЈаё§аёЎ` / `аёўаёӯаё”аёЈаё§аёЎаё„а№ҲаёІаёӘаёҙаёҷаё„а№үаёІвҖҰ` (+) beside its many fee columns. Reading only the fee columns would discard the income leg the file hands us.
- **Actual Net stays internally consistent.** CONTEXT.md already states two things that only reconcile if income is a line: "Actual Net = the total of all signed amounts in the Order's Accounting Entry" *and* "Actual Net = Effective Price minus all platform fees". With a positive `sale_income` line + negative fee lines, `SUM(lines)` equals both вҖ” and equals the Platform's transferred total. A fee-only entry would make `SUM(lines)` negative and force Actual Net to be reconstructed from the Order's price elsewhere, duplicating the source of truth.
- **The transferred-total column becomes a free integrity check.** Because the signed lines must sum to the figure the Platform itself prints as "total transferred", the importer can refuse any row whose categorised lines don't reconcile вҖ” catching a newly-added or renamed fee column the mapping didn't cover, exactly the fail-loud posture of ADR 0005.
- **The rename is cheap now and wrong to defer.** `FeeCategory` was introduced one commit earlier (#61) with no persisted data; holding a `sale_income` value inside a type called *Fee*Category is self-contradictory and would mislead every future reader. Renaming to `AccountingLineCategory` before any importer or report depends on it costs four files.

## Consequences

- `AccountingLineCategory` carries: income/contra side вҖ” `sale_income`, `refund`; fee side вҖ” `commission`, `payment_fee`, `shipping_seller_paid`, `shipping_return`, `marketing_fee`, `affiliate_fee`, `tax_withheld`, `other`. Still extensible. Sign convention unchanged (`+` = seller receives, `вҲ’` = seller pays).
- `actual_net = SUM(all lines across all cycles)` (the #61 denormalisation) is confirmed correct and now equals the Platform's transferred total; reports read the column, never re-derive it.
- Each accounting importer maps **income and fee columns** to lines and **reconciles the line sum against the file's transferred-total column**, fail-loud on a mismatch beyond a 1-satang rounding allowance (ADR 0005). Where a Platform exposes no single transferred-total column, the reconciliation check is skipped for that Platform and the omission is logged (we never invent the total).
- Expected Net / Reconciliation (Phase 6 later slices) compare Expected Net against this Actual Net unchanged; the income line does not alter the comparison, it makes the Actual side complete.
- CONTEXT.md's **Fee Category** term is broadened/renamed to **Accounting Line Category**; the **Accounting Entry** and **Actual Net** terms are clarified to state the income leg explicitly. ADR 0007 is untouched.

## Considered options

- **Entry holds fee lines only; Actual Net = Order's Effective Price + ОЈ fees.** Rejected вҖ” contradicts CONTEXT's "sum of all signed amounts in the entry", makes `SUM(lines)` a negative number that is not Actual Net, splits the Actual figure across two sources, and discards the income/refund columns the file actually provides. No mainstream settlement-reconciliation tool models it this way.
- **Keep the `FeeCategory` name, file income under `other`.** Rejected вҖ” `other` is the catch-all for *unknown fees* (drill-down/triage); overloading it with the gross sale destroys the income/expense split that the P&L breakdown needs and that A2X/Stripe keep distinct.
- **Add income buckets but keep the type named `FeeCategory`.** Rejected вҖ” a type named *Fee* that legitimately holds `sale_income` mis-teaches every future reader; the rename is four files with no data behind it.
